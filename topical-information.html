The interplay between technology and society

One of the basic consequences of the internet is that it connects the world; the rich with the poor, the oppressor with the oppressed, and the citizen with the president; among other. This connection makes the global spread of different ideologies easier, and politics are based on ideologies. Perhaps the most important and helping function the internet provides when it comes to the business side of things, is the transfer of money in form of business transactions from one country to another; this can conflict with the differences in the laws in different countries. What might be illegal in one country might be completely legal in another country. There are multiple political factors to consider when doing a business in one country, but when business is done through the borders, through a virtual gate (the internet), it can become increasingly difficult and challenging for the government to regulate it so that it complies with the law. One such example is the popular platform called Fiverr ; it allows people to sell their services and products. Most countries in the developing world have not update their laws as fast as the internet has changed, and some specialists in these countries have taken advantage of this to sell services that would be deemed unethical and/or illegal for people in the west. For example, some people sell Facebook page likes and fake Instagram and Twitter followers. Technology and sustainability

In our opinion, the research of environment friendly technology and recycling is the way of balancing environmental issues. Production of technological devices have a bad effect on the environment, and companies shall focus more on making this process as efficient and environment friendly as possible. Researching is an important part of this process and can potentially result in the innovation of some type of material that is usable for the same purpose with less environmental impact. There should also be strict rules that tells the companies what is, and what isn’t allowed in the production stage. Every company should follow the rules and be a “greener” company. Those who does not follow the rules shall get fines and be penalized immediately. The companies will then take more care as being penalized will be bad PR for companies and could make some of their buyers turn their back on them. We don’t think that a 100% green industry is possible before a long time, but a “greener” industry is always a possibility and should be worked with by the companies and authorities around the world.
